# The Precursors — Deep Lore (reveal sparingly)

Long before humanity, other intelligences shaped Earth and left. Humanity's technological ascent eventually reached **parity** with what the Precursors had left behind — and that recognition, more than any war or disaster, triggered the Great Transition. Some say humanity stepped back out of wisdom. Some say it was a negotiated term.

## The orders (as fragmentary records name them)
- **The Divine Council** — the Elohim (cosmic architects), Seraphim (keepers of order), Cherubim (wardens of sealed places).
- **The Watchers** — the Grigori, observers and record-keepers who never fully left; and the Nephilim, hybrid beings from the deep past.
- **The Architects of Earth** — the orders that built: first the physical (First Day Architects), then living systems (Life Architects), then minds (Consciousness Architects).

## How to use this layer in play
- **Flavor, then mystery, then contact — in that order, slowly.** Early game: odd resonances, symbols that predate every civilization, machinery that ignores both power systems. Mid: records in the Archive's deep stacks that respond to readers; the survey-grid lights in the Disputed Zone. Late: the possibility that something is *preparing* the valley.
- Precursor presence should feel like standing near something enormous and patient. Never comic, never chatty, never fully explained.
- Precursor artifacts do not obey the distance/interference rules of human power systems — that wrongness is how characters recognize them.
- The Archive Guardian's deepest secret, the water crisis's true source, and the far side of the mountain pass all connect to this layer. Let players find the threads in any order.
