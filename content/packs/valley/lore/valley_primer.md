# The Valley of Echoes — Primer

## The world in one breath
Fifteen years ago humanity completed the **Great Transition**: having achieved technological parity with the ancient Precursor beings, most of the world chose to *step back* — a voluntary de-technologizing. Not collapse; renunciation. What remains is a world where nanite-integrated power systems shaped by old philosophies have replaced industrial machinery, and where the question "how much of what we built should we keep?" divides every community.

## The Valley
The Valley of Echoes is a fertile mountain valley and a microcosm of the whole world's argument. It holds:

- **Millbrook and the valley floor** — unaligned farming communities. Three loose factions: **Traditionalists** (keep technology buried), **Adaptors** (selective, sustainable use), **Explorers** (curious, outward-looking, distrusted by the other two).
- **The Harmonic Heights** — a terraced sonic civilization. Quantum-acoustic technology: buildings that resonate, weather tuned by great chimes, communication by carried tone. Culture prizes patience, tradition, and consonance. Power fades with distance from their resonance matrix.
- **The Radiant Plateau** — a photonic civilization on the opposite rim. Light-guide streets, optical computing, mirage-craft. Culture prizes clarity, revelation, and precision — while being masters of making you see what they choose. Power fades with distance from their photonic array.
- **The Disputed Zone** — the interference band between the two power fields where sound bends light and light carries sound. Lawless, strange, and littered with pre-Transition remains.
- **Archive Hollow** — a recently exposed pre-Transition knowledge vault. The valley's most dangerous political object.

## Current tensions
1. **The Water Crisis** — the Echo River shows spreading contamination. Each civilization suspects the other. The truth is worse.
2. **The Lost Archive** — preserve, study, or seal? Every faction wants a different answer.
3. **The Mountain Pass** — a route out of the valley just opened for the first time in living memory. Fresh boot prints on the far side. Nobody knows whose.

## Tone
Grounded hopeful-strange. Communities genuinely trying to cooperate under strain. Wonder and dread in equal measure at the edges — the Precursor layer should feel *old and vast*, glimpsed, never explained all at once. Violence is possible but always costly and consequential.
