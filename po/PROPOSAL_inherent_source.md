# §1 — THE FLAW, WHICH IS MINE

I defined `natural` as *"not nanite-mediated — body, breath, growth, grief, craft-of-hand."* **That
describes a Somatic exactly and an Ashwarden not at all.**

Palework pulls a living thing's ending forward and kills with it. Ask the Dead gets an answer out of
a corpse. The Grey Road walks a plague ward untouched. **None of that is craft-of-hand, and none of it
is nanite-mediated either.** The category had no room for it, so it went in the only box that was not
"lattice," and the label has been quietly wrong since it shipped.

Look at what is currently sitting together under one word:

| tradition | what it actually does | same kind of thing? |
|---|---|---|
| somatic, marcher, mason | a perfected body, skill at arms, stone that holds | **skill and material** |
| ashwarden, rootkin, threnodist, stillhold | endings pulled forward, growth quickened, feeling as instrument, a binding truce | **metaphysics** |

**Those are not one source. They are two, and only one of them is "natural" in any ordinary sense.**

---

# §2 — ERIK'S ANSWER, AND WHY IT IS BIGGER THAN A FIX

**The Transition was not a retreat from power. It was a change of route to it.**

The Precursor lattice is a **machine for doing what the universe already does.** Nanites are a
middleman — an engineered intermediary that made effects reliable, repeatable, and industrial. When
humanity stepped back, some peoples did not lose those effects. **They learned to reach the same
energies directly**, without the apparatus.

That gives the world three genuine sources instead of two-and-a-catchall:

- **LATTICE** — the machine. Precursor nanites, governed, engineered, reliable.
- **WILD** — the machine ungoverned. The same nanites answering to nobody.
- **INHERENT** — no machine at all. Direct contact with what the universe is doing anyway: endings,
  growth, feeling, stillness, truth. **The oldest way, and the one the lattice was built to replace.**
- **MATERIAL** — the true "natural." Body, skill, stone, edge. Reaches for nothing; needs nothing.
- **COMBINATION** — two of the above at once, first-class (Erik's ratification).

## §2a — And it EXPLAINS Erik's earlier ruling instead of merely restating it

Erik ruled 2026-07-19 that **natural craft BENEFITS from thin lattice** — the Quickwood is the
Rootkin's *best* ground, not their tolerable one. I authored that as a rule and could not say *why*.

**Now it has a mechanism: a dense lattice is INTERFERENCE.** It is a machine sitting between the
practitioner and the thing itself, humming. Thin ground is not "less to compete with" — it is a
**clear signal.** The Ashwarden reads an ending most plainly where the least apparatus stands between
them and it.

**That is a much better world than the one where the Ashwardens simply do not use nanites.** It makes
the Transition a genuine discovery rather than a sacrifice, and it makes the Quickwood's emptiness an
achievement you can feel.

# §3 — WHAT CHANGES MECHANICALLY

**Reclassify 8 traditions:** ashwarden, rootkin, threnodist, stillhold → **inherent**;
somatic, marcher, mason → **material**; verist is the judgement call (§5 Q2).

**And the two behave DIFFERENTLY in the band model, which is the real payoff:**

| source | dense ground | thin ground | shape |
|---|---|---|---|
| lattice | full power | starved | current band |
| **inherent** | **interfered with** | **clearest** | **the lattice band MIRRORED** |
| **material** | **indifferent** | **indifferent** | **FLAT — no band at all** |
| wild | widened variance both ways | widened | SNG-140 |

**This fixes something currently wrong.** `mason` carries band centre **0.32**, which penalises
stonework in dense country. **Why would stone care?** A hammer works in the Gearlands exactly as well
as in the Given Land. Mason should be **flat**, and under the current model it cannot be.

⚠ **This is new curve shape and therefore sits under `tuningNote` — Erik's balance call.** I am not
authoring it without a ruling.

# §4 — IT ALSO CHANGES THE CREATION READING (SNG-192 §6b)

The band-intersection tool gets sharper, not obsolete:

- **material** traditions are flat, so they **never narrow anyone's window.** A Marcher's edge-work
  composes with anything. That is a real and interesting fact about martial builds.
- **inherent + lattice** is the genuinely hard mix — they are *opposed*, not merely different, because
  one is interference to the other. Erik's warning case (ashwarden + enginewright, no common ground)
  becomes not a coincidence of numbers but **a statement about the world.**
- **inherent + material** is the natural coherent build and always has been.

# §4b — ✅ RATIFIED: THE LEANING RULE (Erik, 2026-07-19) — answers Q1 and improves it

> Erik: *"A marcher's material skill by itself is good, but combined with inherent power or
> nanite-infused, it's amazing… but that extra loses something from the pure if in a material place.
> **The pure never loses because it never leaned on anything.**"*

**A craft's band is not a property of its source. It is a measure of what the craft LEANS ON.**

| what it leans on | band | in its ground | out of its ground |
|---|---|---|---|
| **nothing** (pure material) | **FLAT** | good | **good** |
| the universe's own energies (inherent) | mirrored — thin is clear | excellent | diminished |
| the machine (lattice) | standard — dense is powered | excellent | starved |
| **material + an augment** | **inherits the AUGMENT's band** | **amazing** | **worse than pure** |

## The three consequences, and the third is the one worth having

1. **Material is flat only while it is PURE.** Augment a Marcher's edge-work with lattice or inherent
   power and the augmented craft carries that source's band. The material half did not acquire a
   dependency; **the character did.**
2. **Augmentation degrades the baseline — it is a commitment, not an addition.** Technique built
   around an assist assumes the assist. In ground that does not supply it, the augmented Marcher is
   **worse than the pure one**, not merely un-boosted. You cannot lean and also stand as if you never
   leaned.
3. **Pure material's advantage is RELIABILITY, and it is a real one.** Never exceptional, never
   diminished, identical in the Gearlands and the Quickwood and a pocket where reality went blocky.
   **For a world whose whole shape is travel, the craft that never loses is worth more than it looks.**

## Why this is the right rule for THIS world

It makes the Returned's choice legible. **The Quickwood can never match a Lattice-City at full tilt —
and can never be diminished either.** They gave up the ceiling to delete the floor. That is not a
consolation; it is a different and defensible bet, and it is exactly what Erik's *"thin as an
achievement"* has meant all along.

It also gives §6c's braid framing its edge: **a braid buys you a ceiling and sells you your
universality.** Divergence is generative *and* it costs something real. Both halves true at once.

**Canonical line, Erik's words, to carry in the content:** *the pure never loses, because it never
leaned on anything.*

# §4b — ✅ Q1 RULED: material is a FLOOR, not an absence

> Erik 2026-07-19: *"Material should still feel something — but I might be thinking of the combined
> skills. A marcher's material skill by itself is good, but combined with inherent power or
> nanite-infused, it's amazing. But that extra loses something from the pure if in a material place.
> **The pure never loses because it never leaned on anything.**"*

**This is a better model than the one I proposed and it changes the shape of the answer.**

I said material was *flat — no band, indifferent.* That is right about the pure form and it misses
what happens when material is **built on**:

1. **Pure material is FLAT and can never be starved.** A hammer works in the Gearlands exactly as it
   works in the Given Land. **Nothing can be taken from a craft that borrowed nothing.**
2. **Material AUGMENTED — by inherent power or lattice infusion — is better than pure.** That is the
   reason to reach at all, and it is where a Marcher stops being good and becomes remarkable.
3. **The augmentation carries the band. The material underneath does not.**
4. **So in wrong ground, an augmented craft DEGRADES TOWARD ITS PURE FORM** — it does not fail. The
   edge still cuts. You lose the extra, not the craft.

**Material is therefore a floor, not a curve.** It is the only source in the world that guarantees a
practitioner is never helpless anywhere, and it pays for that guarantee with a lower ceiling.

## Why this is the strongest version

It makes each source a genuine and different **bet**, rather than a stat block:

| source | promise | cost |
|---|---|---|
| **material** | never starved, anywhere | lower ceiling |
| **inherent** | clearest where the least apparatus stands between | interfered with in dense country |
| **lattice** | highest power where the machine is thick | starved where it is thin |
| **wild** | widened both ways | unreliable by nature |

And it gives the world a line worth keeping: **the pure never loses because it never leaned on
anything.** That is a fact about the Masons and the Marchers, and it is also the reason someone would
choose to stay pure when augmentation is available.

## §4c — it also improves the creation reading (SNG-192 §6b)

The band-intersection tool should stop reporting **one window** and report **two numbers**:

- **YOUR FLOOR** — what works anywhere, always. Material crafts, and the material component of
  anything augmented.
- **YOUR CEILING** — what works *here*, and where "here" has to be.

*"You can always do this. In thin country you can also do this."* **That is a far better thing to
tell a player than a single density range**, and it makes an augmented build legible as what it is:
a higher ceiling bought with a variable one.
