# §1 — THE IDEA

**A tradition has a ROOT. A school is what that root reaches WITH.**

A Cogitant's root is a **trained mind** — material, flat, never starved. What differs between
Cogitants is what the mind is joined to:

| school of the Cogitants | root | extension | thrives |
|---|---|---|---|
| the mind that touches directly | material | **inherent** | thin country; clearest with least apparatus between |
| the mind that moves the machine | material | **lattice** | dense country; telekinesis as instrumentation |
| the mind that rides the churn | material | **wild** | anywhere, unreliably, both tails widened |

**Same tradition. Same abilities. Three different characters, three different best-grounds, and three
different conversations at a table.**

## Why this is worth building

1. **It is the finest identity the game can offer without one new ability.** "I am a Cogitant" is a
   people. "I am of the school that moves the machine" is a **person**.
2. **It explains variation we have already ratified.** Erik's three authored Ents came out with three
   different dispositions because kind does not determine craft. **Schools are the same principle one
   level down**: tradition does not determine source.
3. **`perAbilityOverrides` in `power_sources.json` is empty by design** and I could not say what would
   ever fill it. **This is the answer, and it fills it at the right level** — a school, not an
   ability-by-ability exception list.
4. **It gives the 22 authored teachers their subject.** Ceriad teaching umbral *in total dark, never
   saying whether you got it right* is not a quirk — **that is a school**, and her students come out
   different from another Umbral master's. A teacher teaches **their** school. That is what makes
   choosing a teacher matter.

---

# §2 — MECHANICS

1. **A character belongs to ONE school per tradition they practise**, chosen at creation or received
   from a teacher.
2. **The school determines the extension source, and therefore the band.** The root's contribution is
   unchanged. Two practitioners of one tradition can have opposite best-grounds.
3. **The floor comes from the root.** A material-rooted tradition (cogitant, threnodist, verist,
   marcher, mason, somatic) is **never helpless anywhere** — per Erik's Q1 ruling, the pure never
   loses. A tradition rooted in inherent or lattice has no such floor, **and that is a real
   difference between peoples worth stating out loud.**
4. **Schools compose with `branch_forks`, they do not replace them.** A fork splits one ability at a
   rank; a school colours how a whole tradition is powered. **Both can apply and they are asking
   different questions.**
5. **A school is learnable, not innate.** Changing schools should be possible, hard, and a story.
6. **Not every tradition needs the same number.** Some have two, some four. **Churnfolk almost
   certainly claim to have none and are lying, or have forty and cannot name them.**

---

# §3 — WHAT THIS DOES FOR CREATION (SNG-192)

**Picking a school is a better first question than picking two abilities.** It is short, it is
characterful, and it drives everything downstream:

- It sets the extension source → which orders the ability pool by fit (§6b)
- It sets the band → which produces the floor/ceiling reading (§4c of the proposal)
- It names a teacher who could teach you → which turns the last screen into a hook
- **And it is answerable by someone who has read nothing**, which is the whole problem with a
  45-button wall

*"Do you reach for the world, work the machine, or ride the churn?"* — three sentences, and the
build follows.

---

# §4 — INVARIANTS

1. **A school never adds abilities.** It changes how the same craft is powered. **The moment a school
   grants exclusive abilities it becomes a subclass, and the tradition fractures.**
2. **Every school is viable somewhere.** No school is a trap; each has country where it is strongest.
3. **The root is the tradition's, not the school's.** A school may not change what a people
   fundamentally is.
4. **Schools are named from inside the culture** — what its practitioners call themselves, not a
   taxonomy label.
5. **A character may practise two traditions with different schools.** That is a braid (SNG-192 §6c),
   and it should be legible as one.
