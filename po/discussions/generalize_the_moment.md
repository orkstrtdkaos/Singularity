Erik, seeing the braid celebration ("Undying Ledger" mint — the ceremonial card, the tier badge, the
rank-1 grant, "rename this braid"): wants that kind of moment for OTHER earned thresholds — relationship
milestones, teacher secured, quest advances. Parked for later.

## Why the braid moment works (the principle to preserve)
It marks something **earned and irreversible** — 40 co-activations, a craft neither parent could do alone,
a threshold crossed and made ceremonial. If everything gets a celebration, nothing does. So the design
question is not "reuse the visual" but **"which moments are actually thresholds."**

## The discrimination (Erik's three examples are NOT equivalent)
- **Relationship milestones — YES, strongest fit.** Crossing friendly→ally→devoted, a bond-type shift
  (someone becomes a mentor, a companion reaches a stage) is braid-shaped: earned over time, a real
  threshold, changes what's possible after. Pairs with the parked one-way-ratchet audit — a relationship
  that can be LOST makes reaching a milestone mean something, and the moment is the reward for the climb.
  **Build first.**
- **Teacher secured — YES, most deserving, already designed as a scene.** The ashwarden tradition arc's
  "Proving → teacher commits" beat and "capstone learned as a scene, not a menu unlock" are already specced
  (SNG-203 §4). This is just SNG-197's moment doctrine applied to the capstone/teacher beat the tradition
  arc already calls for. Neth committing should land like the braid does.
- **Quest advances — MOSTLY NO (the important discrimination).** A quest STAGE advancing is routine — the
  normal texture of play. Celebrate every tick and the celebration becomes wallpaper, killing what makes the
  braid special. But a quest RESOLVING — a world-arc quest moving the shared clock, a branching outcome that
  forecloses a path — IS a threshold. Rule: **endings and irreversible forks get moments; steps don't.**

## The principle, in one sentence
**A moment fires when you cross a threshold you can't uncross — not when you make progress toward one.**
- FIRES: braid mint · relationship band-up · teacher committed · capstone learned · quest RESOLVED · arc
  stage advancing on the shared clock · companion reaching a stage.
- DOES NOT: a use practiced · a stage stepped · a fetch delivered · rapport ticking +1.

## The engineering economy (why this is cheaper than it looks)
SNG-197 part 2 ALREADY built the moment machinery — the beat, the ceremonial surface, the "here's what you
crossed" framing, the optional rename. This is not four celebrations; it's **one MOMENT-TYPE built once and
fired from several threshold-events**, each with its own content (a band-up moment reads differently from a
braid mint) but the same ceremonial spine. Generalize the moment into a thing the engine can RAISE, don't
reskin the braid card repeatedly.

## The guard that keeps it special
**A rarity budget.** The braid lands hard partly because it's rare. Six moments a session and the seventh is
noise. The generalized moment-type needs a governor — same as gambit surfacing and legend deployment — so
even legitimate thresholds don't stack into a parade. A great moment twice a session beats a good one every
scene.
