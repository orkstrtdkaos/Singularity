# Results — SNG-080 The world must PUSH

Date: 2026-07-12 · v1.8.36 · npm test green · live-verified the map on a fresh port. Status: **shipped, awaiting Erik leg.** Only Aevi closes.

## Diagnosis (as the spec framed it)
Erik sat in Millbrook and nothing happened. Millbrook is the **safest place in the world** — zero fight-capable encounters are eligible in a farming village (correct). The real defect: **the world is REACTIVE — it waits for him.** Nothing pushes, nothing arrives, and the map doesn't say where danger lives.

## The fix — the pacing rule (the actually-missing thing)
- **New pure `engine/pacing.js`:** `pressureTier(quietTurns, streak)` — under the threshold (3) the world stays quiet; at the threshold it ACTS (tier 1); an **ignored** world **escalates** (the streak raises the tier, capped at 4): rumor → someone with a problem → a hook that won't wait → **something arrives**. `isEventfulTurn` resets the count on any real beat.
- **`pressureDirective(tier, dangerLevel, questTitles)`** is **register/danger-aware**: a SAFE place gets *"a frightened neighbour, a rumor off the road — NEVER a bandit ambush here"*; a dangerous place *"can have teeth."* And it **tightens a live quest thread** — *"the antagonist acts on their own clock whether or not the player does; a thread ignored gets worse and comes closer"* — which folds in the spec's "the villain acts" and "hooks come to him" cheaply, using the quest context already in the GM prompt.
- **`app.js maybeWorldPressure`** (in `applyTurn`): counts quiet turns, **resets on any eventful beat** (an encounter live/woven, a quest change, a scene end), suppresses during a gambit, and hands the **next** GM turn a pressure directive; spaced by resetting the counter after each push so the world pushes without burying the player.
- **`gm.js`** renders it as `## THE WORLD ACTS` — woven into the fiction, never a system announcement.

## Danger, made findable on the map
The map now shows danger **graduated across all 5 tiers** (safe · quiet · uneasy · dangerous · deadly) — a coloured **ring** on each node (dl≥2) and a **chip** in the detail panel — so the player can SEE where fighting lives. The Disputed Zone on Millbrook's doorstep is now legible and inviting instead of a silent unknown.

## Verification
- `npm test` green — 6 pacing checks: threshold behaviour, escalation-while-ignored (capped), eventful-resets, a SAFE-place directive (human/small, never an ambush, tightens the thread), a DANGEROUS-place directive (teeth, tier-4 "arrives").
- **Live-verified on a fresh port:** the world map renders danger **graduated across 92 nodes** — dl0=4 (safe valley-floor, incl. Millbrook) · dl1=17 · dl2=30 · dl3=21 · dl4=20 — with 71 danger rings. No console errors.

## spec_boundaries
- **`dangerLevel` audit (CCode's job per the spec):** 4 of 92 locations lack the field — `millbrook`, `echo_river_crossing`, `harmonic_heights_terrace`, `radiant_plateau_edge` — all valley-floor safe places. They floor to 0 ("safe"), which is **correct** for them, and the map handles the default gracefully. Adding the field is Aevi's content lane; flagged, not edited. (If Aevi wants CI to require it, that's a one-line `content_ci` check once the 4 fields are authored — I left it out so the build doesn't fail on content I don't own.)
- **The villain-clock is driven through the GM directive**, not a separate autonomous escalation engine. The pressure directive tells the GM to tighten a live thread on quiet turns; a fully autonomous "Grael advances the ledger while you're away even with no GM turn" would be a `worldtick` extension (the boundary is here, the deeper build is a follow-on).
- **A propagating consequence surfacing as a full SCENE** (vs the away-digest line) is likewise reachable via the same pressure hook (tier-4 "something arrives"); a dedicated worldtick→scene promotion is a follow-on.
