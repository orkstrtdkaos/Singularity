## SNG-082 — the world map: pan/zoom + real terrain
Erik: *"this is really awesome… but I can't move the map around or zoom in"* — and it holds 92 locations across 24 regions.
- **Pan/zoom** — the map now reuses the SNG-054 viewport (wrapped in `#skill-svg` + `.graph-vp`): **scroll to zoom, drag to pan, ⤢ fit, and ◎ centre-on-me** (pans + zooms to the current location). Reset on Back.
- **Real terrain** from the authored `content/packs/core/rules/regions.json` (loaded into `CONTENT.regions`): each region drawn as a **palette-filled hull** of its locations — new pure `convexHull` + `regionShape` (worldmap.js), the hull padded outward so terrain wraps the nodes with a soft margin. The Valley renders its authored `#3d4a3a` green; every region carries its own `base/accent/edge`. Roads are the `connections`; **the Axis Gate's twelve roads draw heaviest (the spine)**. The three regions that must **look wrong** do (pattern-reach dashed-to-nothing, veiled-reach doubled, numinous-reach uncertain); the expanding borders (the Blaze `radiant_wastes`, the Churn Edge `unspooling`) show a creeping dashed edge. Terrain is **derived, not an image**, so a generated location inherits the right ground.
- **Verified live:** 24 region hulls (Valley filled `#3d4a3a`), 24 labels, 28 spine roads, 3 wrong regions, all 4 zoom controls + the viewport; zoom transforms correctly (1 → 1.56 → 2.2-centred → reset); no console errors. 3 hull-geometry smoke checks.
