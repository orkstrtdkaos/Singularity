## The three doors
Creation now opens with three cards (describe first): **✎ Describe yourself** · **▶ Play the opening** (prologue) · **⚡ Quick start** (the ring picker). The describe door is the gentlest — for players who will not play a prologue to configure a character and will not study a 24-point ring.

## What it does
1. **`gm.js suggestBuild()`** — free-text description + compact catalogs (the ring: id · people · pole · craft · antipode · neighbours; folk crafts; backgrounds; starting companions) → structured JSON. The **model proposes** primary/secondary/tertiary each as `{traditionId, why, cost}`, plus origin, background, form, companion, folk crafts, and non-domains — but it is handed only real ids and **never owns ring geometry.**
2. **`renderDescribeDoor`** — name + a free-text "who are you?" box. Empty → a gentle nudge. On submit: builds the catalogs from loaded content, calls `suggestBuild`, and on any failure falls through to a friendly retry pointing at the other two doors (never blocks).
3. **`sanitizeSuggestedDomains`** (Design Law 1) — the engine **validates every id against the ring and enforces the geometry itself**: drops any non-tradition/folk id; nulls a secondary equal to the primary or its antipode; nulls a tertiary that isn't a ring-neighbour of the secondary, or that collides with a chosen pole or a closed antipode.
4. **`renderDescribeReveal`** — the payoff. Draws the great circle with the **chosen poles lit and their antipodes darkened** (the dashed axis across the ring). For each domain: the people · the model's *why* · **"Closes X"** where X is the antipode **the engine computed from the ring** (not the model's prose) · the model's *cost* line. States the **primary antipode as closed forever** ("Rootkin is closed to you forever — only a cross-pole braid ever crosses it"). Surfaces origin/background/form/companion, names free **folk crafts** ("Hunter — free, open to all"), and is honest about **what needs no domain** ("romantic isn't a domain — how you play, not a pole"). Buttons: **Rewrite** · **Adjust on the circle** (hands the suggestion into the standard picker, seeded) · **Yes — this is who I am** (→ the existing domain-filtered ability step, then the normal tail).

## spec_boundaries
- **Attributes** use the balanced default spread (12 pts, 3/3/3/3) — the describe door skips manual point allocation for gentleness; they grow through play, and the Quick-start door still offers manual allocation. If Aevi wants the description to *shape* the spread (a "very smart" character starting higher Reason), that's a clean follow-on in `suggestBuild`.
- **Companion** is suggested and pre-selected but the player still passes through the companion step to confirm/name (unchanged flow) — the suggestion doesn't skip the step.
- The reveal reuses `domainCircleSVG` (non-selectable); a future nicety is animating the antipode axis or a tooltip on the closed pole, but the legibility target ("light position, darken antipode") is met.
- Content lane untouched: `suggestBuild` reads `traditions.json`/`origins.json`/`backgrounds`/companions as authored; no content edited.
