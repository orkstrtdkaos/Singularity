## ⭐ AMENDMENT 1 (Erik, 2026-07-13) — the substrate must be TWO-SIDED, not a fuel gauge
**The authored rule is one-sided and it doesn't track.** `theRule` = *"effective power scales with `min(density + carried, 1.0)` against dependency."* That is **monotonic in density — more substrate never hurts.** Consequence: a low-dependency tradition (Rootkin 0.18, Somatic 0.2) runs at ~100% **everywhere**, and *better* in dense ground. The Returned get a ceiling penalty but **no hostile terrain**. That breaks the headline promise ("two difficulty maps, both personal") — only the Continuous actually have a second map.

**Erik's correction:** a life/nature craft should take an **equivalent (NOT exactly equal) penalty in high-machine / cities / dense-lattice ground.** The dense lattice is *hostile* to the organic modality — interference, not fuel.

**The model this implies — an AFFINITY BAND, not a gauge.** Each tradition has a preferred substrate level; effective output falls off as a place departs from it **in either direction**:
- **Continuous** (high dependency) — affinity HIGH. Below it = **starvation**, and the falloff is **steep/catastrophic** (they run on borrowed lattice; a Seraph in the Quickwood ≈ 13%).
- **Returned** (low dependency) — affinity LOW. Above it = **interference**, and the falloff is **milder but real** (a Rootkin in the Gearlands is impaired and noisy, not switched off — floor it around 60–75%, never 13%).
- **"Equivalent, not exactly equal" = an ASYMMETRIC curve:** below-affinity (starvation) drops hard; above-affinity (interference) drops soft. Same idea (mismatch costs), different teeth.
- **Affinity can largely be DERIVED from the existing `substrateDependency`** (high dep → high affinity), so Aevi may not need a whole new table — but the *authoring judgement* (how soft the interference side is per people) is Aevi's, and it must be **tuned with the balance harness, never eyeballed.**
- **Nice emergent from carried substrate under this model:** `density + carried` pushes a Returned craft *further above* its affinity in dense ground, so a Rootkin carrying a battery into the Gearlands makes it *worse* — which is exactly why *"the Rootkin find the whole trade faintly ridiculous."* The Waystaff rescues the Continuous from starvation; it cannot rescue the Returned from interference. Two-sided falls out clean.

**→ This is the load-bearing change. Aevi should re-author `theRule` (and the interference-side softness per people) before build.**

## FINDING 2 — there is NO "power" scalar in the resolve chain to multiply
The spec's mental model ("effective power scales… 13% of ceiling") is multiplicative-power. **The engine has no power scalar.** `resolve.js successChance` is a single **additive success-CHANCE** accumulator (attribute + skill + abilityLevel + spectral + aptitudes + equipment ± novelty ± exhaustion − difficulty), clamped to `[floorChance, ceilingChance]`, then a d100 threshold roll. There is nothing to multiply by 0.13.

**The clean slots for the substrate factor (all additive-model-native):**
1. **A success-chance penalty** — `chance -= substratePenalty` (exactly how `difficulty`, `exhaustedPenalty`, and `spectral` already attach). "Nearly OFF" = a penalty large enough to hit `floorChance`. **This is the primary slot — one line in `successChance`, ability actions only.**
2. **An energy-cost multiplier** — thin substrate makes a craft *strain* to reach the lattice: `applyEnergyCost × substrateMult`. Composes cleanly and adds the logistics feel (you burn out faster where the lattice is thin).
3. **A hard gate** — below a floor of effective substrate, an ability is simply **unavailable** (not offered / refused with a reason). This is what "nearly OFF" should *feel* like at the extreme, and it's cleaner than a 3%-chance cast.
4. **⛔ ABILITY ACTIONS ONLY.** A weapon swing (`abilityId: null`) is substrate-independent — attributes + steel. This is precisely SNG-089's narrative baseline ("no tradition is ever helpless"); the two features must agree, and the natural key is *"does this action use a craft ability?"*.

Recommended: **(1) + (3)** for the core feel (penalty scaling to a hard gate at the extreme), **(2)** as a follow-on for the logistics layer.

## FINDING 3 — the SNG-079 collision is REAL and already half-present. Keep them SEPARATE terms.
`successChance` **already** contains a "does the place help?" term: `spectrumAlignment(location.spectrum, action.axes) × sm.locationWeight`. That is dispositional fit — **SNG-079's territory already lives in the resolve chain.** So SNG-079 (spectral/dispositional fit) and SNG-090 (substrate density) both modify chance based on the *place*, and they will **double-count if merged.**

They are **orthogonal physical facts** and must be **two separate, separately-clamped terms**, summed:
- **SNG-079 spectral fit** = does the place's *disposition* (dark/light, order/chaos…) agree with what you're *doing*.
- **SNG-090 substrate** = can your craft *run at all* here (is there enough lattice for your dependency, and is it not so dense it drowns your organic modality).
- A place can be dispositionally perfect **and** substrate-dead (or dispositionally hostile **and** substrate-rich). The Seraph-in-Quickwood is switched off by *substrate* regardless of disposition. **Do not fold substrate into the spectrum term.** Document the boundary in SYSTEM_SPEC §resolve so a future edit can't quietly conflate them.

## FINDING 4 — density is per-REGION; the chain runs per-LOCATION
`the_substrate.json substrateDensity` is authored for 24 **regions**; the resolve chain resolves per **location**. Build needs: each location derives density from its `regionId` (default), with an optional per-location override (a lattice-vault inside a natural region, or a dead cell inside a city). **CI: require an effective density resolvable for every location** (same spirit as the connectivity checks). Small, loadable wiring. `the_substrate.json` is **not yet loaded** (state.js loads the *generative* substrate, a different file) — that load is part of the build.

## FINDING 5 — "tell the player" is non-negotiable (SNG-084)
A silent success-chance penalty is the cruellest possible bug. The build MUST surface it: a **receipt line** (like `affinityReceipt`: *"The lattice is thin here — your craft runs at a fraction"* / *"The lattice is dense and hostile to your green craft — it fights you"*), a **GM context line** so narration matches, and the **map overlay** (substrate beside danger — "two difficulty maps, both personal"). Non-optional per the spec.
